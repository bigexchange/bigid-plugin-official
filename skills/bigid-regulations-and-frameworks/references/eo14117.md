# EO 14117 — Reference File

Regulation/framework reference for the BigID Regulations & Frameworks skill. Loaded in Step 4 when the
user selects **EO 14117**. Provides the regulatory facts, BigID category mapping,
controls-mapping skeleton, and report outline the PDF generator consumes.

US Executive Order **14117** — *Preventing Access to Americans' Bulk Sensitive Personal
Data and United States Government-Related Data by Countries of Concern* — issued
February 28, 2024, implemented by the DOJ National Security Division's Final Rule
("Data Security Program" or "DSP"), codified at 28 C.F.R. Part 202.

The report is written for a CISO, CDO, or General Counsel at a US person or US entity
that holds bulk sensitive personal data or US-government-related data.

---

## Hardcoded regulatory facts

- **Instrument:** Executive Order 14117 (Feb 28, 2024); DOJ Final Rule effective
  **April 8, 2025**; DOJ enforcement of affirmative compliance obligations began
  **October 6, 2025** (after the 270-day grace window for the due-diligence, audit,
  and reporting requirements).
- **Regulator:** US Department of Justice — National Security Division (DOJ NSD). The
  DSP incorporates by reference the CISA *Security Requirements for Restricted
  Transactions* (Final Edition, 2024).
- **Applies to:** US persons (citizens, lawful permanent residents, US-organized
  entities, and persons in the US) engaging in covered data transactions.
- **Countries of concern:** China (including Hong Kong and Macau), Cuba, Iran, North
  Korea, Russia, Venezuela.
- **Covered data categories** (with bulk thresholds):
  - Human ʻomic data (e.g., genomic, epigenomic, proteomic) — 100 US persons
  - Biometric identifiers — 1,000 US persons
  - Precise geolocation data — 1,000 US devices
  - Personal health data — 10,000 US persons
  - Personal financial data — 10,000 US persons
  - Covered personal identifiers (CPI) — 100,000 US persons
  - US government-related data — any quantity (no threshold)
- **Transaction tiers:**
  - **Prohibited transactions** — data-brokerage transactions and bulk human ʻomic or
    biospecimen transactions with countries of concern or covered persons.
  - **Restricted transactions** — vendor, employment, and investment agreements
    permitted only if the US person implements the CISA security requirements,
    maintains a data compliance program, completes an annual independent audit, and
    files annual reports.
- **Penalties:** civil monetary penalties up to the greater of ~$368,000 per violation
  or twice the value of the transaction; criminal penalties up to $1,000,000 and/or 20
  years' imprisonment for willful violations (IEEPA-based).
- **Audit & reporting:** restricted-transaction parties must maintain a written data
  compliance program (Subpart J), conduct an annual independent audit, and file an
  annual report to DOJ NSD.

## Report section content (EO 14117)

Use the skill's **fixed nine canonical sections and headings** (Step 5 Repeatability
Contract). Headings stay verbatim; content below is EO 14117-specific.

1. **Cover page** — title "EO 14117 — Data Security Program Compliance Report";
   subtitle "Bulk Sensitive Data & Government-Related Data Assessment"; org name if
   known; date; classification "Internal — Confidential"; data source "BigID MCP".
2. **Executive Summary** — overall posture; total open cases; per-class bulk-threshold
   exposure (any covered class within reach of its threshold); Compliant / Partially
   compliant / Non-compliant rollup. Fold in any Step 3h currency-check finding — particularly
   covered-persons additions or new DOJ guidance.
3. **Regulatory Overview** — what the EO and DSP require; the prohibited vs restricted
   distinction; countries of concern; covered data classes and bulk thresholds;
   enforcement state (DSP effective Apr 8 2025; affirmative obligations Oct 6 2025);
   CISA security requirements incorporated by reference.
4. **Data Landscape** — BigID categories mapped to EO 14117 covered classes; per-class
   counts vs bulk thresholds (flag any class that exceeds its threshold); volume of
   government-related data (any quantity matters).
5. **Compliance Frameworks Active** — frameworks, control counts, enabled status (3a).
6. **Policy & Security Posture** — policy pass/fail (3b), cases by severity (3c),
   top critical cases (3d).
7. **Controls Compliance Mapping** — the heart of the report. Use the controls skeleton
   above. For each control area: DSP/CISA expectation → finding → gap → status →
   reference. Use the §7 table columns from the Repeatability Contract.
8. **Open Items & Remediation Plan** — Immediate (0–30) / Short-term (30–90) /
   Medium-term (90–180). Prioritize: bulk-threshold breaches with countries of concern
   first; CISA org-level controls (IAM, secret management) second; data-level controls
   (minimization, masking) third.
9. **Conclusion & Attestation** — Domain/Area | Status | Key gap rollup; 1–2 paragraphs
   on what non-compliance means in DOJ-NSD enforcement terms (civil + criminal exposure
   under IEEPA); numbered attestation list citing live data.

### Tone and voice

- Write as a national-security-aware compliance professional, not a marketer.
- Be direct about whether bulk thresholds are met or near-met for any covered class —
  this is the trigger for DSP applicability.
- Connect technical findings to DSP/CISA language explicitly (e.g., "unmasked CPI at
  rest fails CISA §3.4 data-level controls expected of restricted transaction parties").
- Note that compliance program documentation (§202.1001 et seq.) and the annual
  independent audit live outside BigID — flag them clearly as gaps if absent.
